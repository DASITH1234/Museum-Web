The Venus de Milo

Milos, Greek, 130-100 BCE

Standing in front of you is a tall marble statue of the Greek goddess Aphrodite, a little over six feet high. She is standing in a relaxed pose, with most of her weight on one leg, giving her body a gentle curve. The upper part of her body is uncovered, while the lower half is wrapped in thick stone fabric that looks soft and heavy, as if it could slide down. Both of her arms are missing, which is one of the most noticeable things about the statue. Her hair is tied back neatly, and her face looks calm and emotionless. The white marble feels cool and smooth, and the carved folds of the fabric create deep shadows that make the statue feel alive.
