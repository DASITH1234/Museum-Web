The Winged Victory of Samothrace

Samothrase, Greek, 200-190 BCE

This dramatic sculpture stands at the top of a grand staircase, commanding the space like a ship's figurehead. It depicts the goddess Nike landing on the prow of a stone warship. She is headless and armless, but her body is full of explosive energy. Her massive, feathered wings extend high behind herâ€”the right wing is an original marble piece, while the left is a plaster reconstruction. She leans forward into a strong imaginary wind that presses her thin, wet-looking clothing against her stomach and legs, revealing the female form underneath. The fabric ripples and flaps violently behind her, freezing a moment of triumphant motion in solid stone.
